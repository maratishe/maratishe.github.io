
Mobiquitous 2021 Call for Workshops

Mobiquitous is a leading international conference on Mobile and Ubiquitous
Computing, which covers a wide range of research topics from theory to
applications. The 13th Annual International Conference on Mobile and Ubiquitous
Systems: Computing, Networking and Services (Mobiquitous 2021) will be
organized in Beppu, Oita, Japan, from 8-11th November 2021.

Mobiquitous 2021 plans to host several workshops. The organization Committee
invites proposals for one-day or half-day workshops, which are held prior to
the main conference, i.e., 8th, November 2021. The purpose of workshops is to
promote lively interaction and discussion among participants and activate the
main conference. Additionally, workshops also aim to deepen several technical
topics and foster the expertise of researchers. Some of the potential topics
treated in workshops are listed below, but not limited to:

- Wireless Access Technologies
- Networked Sensing and Applications
- Mobile Device Architectures
- Mobile Systems and Applications
- Mobile Data Management and Analytics
- Mobile Multimedia
- Mobile User Interfaces and Interaction Technologies
- Mobile User Experience
- Toolkit, and Languages for Mobile Computing
- Energy Aware Mobile Computing
- Mobile Cloud and Edge Computing
- Distributed Machine Learning
- Semantic Web Technologies
- Localization and Tracking
- Internet of Things
- Crowdsourcing
- Participatory Sensing
- Social Network Applications to Mobile Computing
- Context and Location Aware Applications and Services
- Wearable Computing
- Body Area Networks
- Security and Privacy
- Vehicular Networks and Computing

The workshops may include a mix of regular papers, invited presentations,
tutorials, and panels that promote interaction and discussion among
participants, and deepen the expertise of them. Plans to promote those
objectives should be clearly addressed in workshop proposals. The workshop
papers are to be published along with the main conference papers as an adjunct
publication from Springer.


***
*** Format of the Proposal
***

Each workshop proposal (maximum 5 pages) must include:

- Title and acronym of the workshop.
- Description of the technical issues covered, emphasizing the timeliness of
the proposed workshop (1 page maximum)
- Workshop Organizers (names, affiliation and contact information)
- A short biography of the organizers (up to 200 words)
- Names of potential participants, such as program committee members and invited speakers
- Planned format of the workshop, including:
+ Duration of the workshop: Half-day, Full-day and tentative schedule
+ Number of refereed papers, hot topic sessions, keynotes, panels, tutorials
+ Draft Call for Papers
+ A description of the publicity and promotion plan
+ Workshop potential website address (if available at the time of the proposal
will be required later if the workshop proposal is accepted)
- If appropriate, a description of past versions of the workshop, including the
number of submitted and accepted papers, number of attendees, etc.

Please address questions to Workshop Chairs:
Janick Edinger, Md Osman Gani, and Takuya Yoshihiro by sending e-mail to
mobiquitous2021ws@ml.sys.wakayama-u.ac.jp.
